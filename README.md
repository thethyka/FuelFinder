# Fuel Finder

**Find the cheapest fuel along your drive — and never run out on the way.**

Fuel Finder plans your trip from A to B, then tells you whether you need to fill
up, where the best-value petrol station is along the way, and how much you'll
spend. It's built for Australian drivers, who own roughly 1.8 cars per household
and feel every cent at the bowser.

## Why it's useful

Most maps apps get you from one place to another, but they don't think about your
fuel tank or your wallet. Fuel Finder does both:

- **Saves you money** — it checks live petrol prices near your route and points
  you to the cheapest station, not just the closest one.
- **Keeps you moving** — it works out whether your current tank can make the
  trip. If it can't, it adds a fuel stop before you'd run dry.
- **Knows your car** — tell it your fuel efficiency, tank size, and how much fuel
  you have right now, and it does the maths for you.
- **Applies your discounts** — tick the loyalty cards you hold (like RAC or
  Woolworths Rewards) and prices are adjusted automatically.

## How to use it

1. **Draw your route.** Set your starting point and destination using the
   directions panel — type the addresses or click on the map.
2. **Tell us about your car.** Pick your fuel type (Unleaded 91, Premium 95,
   Premium 98, or Diesel), then enter:
   - your **fuel efficiency** (litres per 100 km — e.g. `10`)
   - your **tank capacity** (litres — e.g. `60`)
   - your **current fuel level** (litres — e.g. `15`)
3. **Add your discounts.** Tick any loyalty or membership cards you have.
4. **Press Calculate.** Fuel Finder draws your route, and if a top-up is needed,
   marks the best-value station to stop at along the way.

Not sure what your fuel efficiency is? There's a built-in helper to point you in
the right direction — most cars list it in the owner's manual or on the
manufacturer's website.

## Where the prices come from

Fuel Finder uses **live, real-world fuel prices** pulled in fresh each time you
search, so the recommendation reflects what stations are actually charging — not
yesterday's numbers.

## Good to know

- Prices and stations update constantly, so what you see is a snapshot for right
  now. Always confirm the price at the pump.
- The fuel-stop suggestion is a guide to help you avoid running low — use your
  own judgement on when and where to fill up.
